I use AI tools every day. Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and ChatGPT are all part of how I work through research, writing, and problem-solving. I have been doing this long enough now to know what these tools are actually good for and where they fall short.

This is not a post about AI replacing work. It is about what changes when you treat these tools as a serious part of your process rather than a shortcut.

## The Tools and What I Use Them For

**Perplexity** is where most research starts. It pulls from current sources and cites them, which matters when you are working on anything where accuracy is on the line. If I need to understand market size, recent industry moves, or what a company has actually said publicly, Perplexity gets me there faster than a raw search and gives me something to verify against.

**Claude** is where I do most of my thinking work. Long-form writing, strategic analysis, working through a problem out loud. Claude handles nuance better than most tools I have used. It pushes back when something does not make sense, which is useful when you are trying to stress-test an idea rather than just get validation.

**Gemini** fills in where the other two have gaps, particularly for document work and anything tied into the Google ecosystem. It is also a useful second opinion when I want to see how a different model frames the same question.

**ChatGPT** was the first AI tool I used professionally. At Tanbii, I used it to draft community announcements, write engagement prompts, and produce event copy. It cut production time significantly. That experience taught me the most important lesson about working with AI: the output is only as good as the judgment you bring to it. ChatGPT produced drafts. I decided what was right for the community.

## What Actually Changes When You Use These Tools Well

The biggest shift is not speed, though that matters. It is that you can engage with harder problems earlier.

Before AI tools, researching a competitive landscape, drafting a strategy document, and writing the communication around it were three separate time investments. Now I can move through all three in the same session, which means I spend more time on the decisions and less time on the groundwork before I can even get to the decisions.

For someone in entertainment business, where the landscape changes fast and being informed is a competitive advantage, that matters.

## What These Tools Do Not Do

They do not replace judgment. Every piece of research still needs verification. Every draft still needs a read from someone who understands the context. Every strategic recommendation still needs to be stress-tested against what you actually know about the market.

I have seen people use AI tools and produce work that sounds authoritative but has no real grounding. That is the misuse. The tools amplify what you bring to them. If you bring shallow thinking, you get faster shallow output. If you bring real knowledge and clear questions, you get something you can actually use.

## Why This Matters for Entertainment Business

The entertainment industry runs on information and relationships. Being able to research faster, write clearer, and think through problems more thoroughly is a direct professional advantage.

It also means being able to take on more ambitious work. The Tempest eSports brand strategy I developed during my Masters program required competitive analysis, audience research, positioning frameworks, and content strategy. AI tools made it possible to go deep on all of those without the research phase eating the entire timeline.

That is the model: use the tools to go further, not to do less.

## The Honest Take

I am better at my work because of these tools. Not because they do the work for me, but because they give me more room to think. The judgment, the industry knowledge, and the ability to know when something is wrong, those still have to come from somewhere. For me, they come from the experience, the coursework, and paying close attention to the industry I want to work in.

The tools are part of the workflow. They are not the whole thing.
